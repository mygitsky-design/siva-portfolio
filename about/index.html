How I work

Starting with where the complexity comes from

I tend to look at product problems beyond the immediate screen. I first try to understand why the problem exists, who is affected, and what constraints the team is working with. Often the issue isn't the interface itself; it's people working around limitations in the product, and those workarounds creating more dependency on internal teams. I bring the recurring issues together, work with product and engineering to define a practical direction, and stage the work so the team can deliver it in phases.

My background as an individual contributor shapes how I lead. I stay close to the research and to the detail of the interface, and I work closely with engineering, because the constraints they raise usually improve the design rather than limit it. I care about systems, accessibility, and product quality because they are what keep a product usable once it has grown past its first version.

AI has changed how I work day to day, mostly in research synthesis, faster prototyping, and validating a direction earlier. The problems I want to work on are the ones where an enterprise product has become difficult to use as it scaled, where the answer is as much about strategy and systems as it is about screens.
