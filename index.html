Design Leader

I turn organizational complexity into product strategy.

I lead design for enterprise SaaS products, usually ones that have grown complex enough that teams end up working around them. I try to understand where that complexity comes from, help product and engineering agree on what to address first, and stay close to execution through delivery. A large part of that work now is finding where AI meaningfully reduces friction rather than adding another feature.

What I lead

Where I spend my time as a design leader: strategy, systems, and outcomes teams can measure.

AI-native product development

Finding the specific places where AI reduces real friction, such as inline source creation inside a legacy enterprise workflow, and building it into the flow rather than adding it on the side.

Design systems & standards

I treat reliability, versioning, and validation as design problems, so teams keep shipping consistent, error-resistant experiences as the product scales.

UX strategy & research

Mixed-method research with admins and executives, synthesised into evidence-backed, vote-ranked roadmaps the team can act on.

Accessibility

Accessibility treated as a baseline of product quality, built into the design system and its governance so it holds up as the product grows.

Cross-functional leadership

Aligning product, engineering, and technical-communication leaders on the same problem and a single roadmap, which is often where the real difficulty lies.

Team building & mentoring

Managing and growing UX teams, mentoring through regular 1:1s, and keeping projects and stakeholders aligned as the team scales.

Selected work, Enterprise design leadership

A ground-up SaaS platform rebuild and a company-wide accessibility transformation, spanning product strategy, design systems, and organizational change.
